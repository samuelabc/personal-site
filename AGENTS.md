## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience is mixed, with emphasis on personal interest and self-expression while still being useful for recruiters, hiring managers, and engineering peers. Visitors should quickly understand who Samuel is, what he builds, and how to start a conversation.

### Brand Personality
Bold, playful, and experimental. The tone should feel confident and human, not stiff or corporate. Emotional goal: spark curiosity and memorability while still signaling technical competence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial + modern brutalist in light mode. Keep the current warm-paper palette, high-contrast borders, expressive typography, and tactile interaction style. Maintain a handcrafted feel and avoid generic SaaS visual patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize personality with purpose: each visual flourish should strengthen identity and clarity, not distract from content.
2. Showcase craft through interaction: use motion and micro-interactions to communicate quality and confidence, while respecting reduced-motion users.
3. Keep hierarchy unmistakable: make projects, experience, and contact pathways obvious within the first scroll.
4. Preserve editorial tension: combine strict structure (grids, rules, uppercase labels) with expressive accents (serif italics, color pops, dynamic details).
5. Avoid template aesthetics: prefer distinctive compositions and details over common startup-landing-page conventions.
