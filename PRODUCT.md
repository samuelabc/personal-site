# Product Context

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio site for Samuel Thien, a full stack engineer based in Kuala Lumpur. The site communicates who Samuel is as a person and builder, and makes it easy for visitors to understand his work and start a conversation.

## Users

- **Primary:** People curious about Samuel: recruiters, hiring managers, engineering peers, potential collaborators
- **Secondary:** Samuel himself, as a creative outlet and professional anchor

Visitors arrive with varying intent: some scanning quickly for competence signals, others exploring out of genuine interest. The site should reward both.

## Brand Voice

Warm, curious, grounded. Like talking to someone who genuinely loves what they do. Not corporate, not performing confidence. Honest and clear.

## Register

Brand. Design IS the product.

## Aesthetic Anchor

"Illustrated field journal." Organic, warm, lived-in, distinctly human. The feeling of flipping through a notebook near an open window in afternoon sunlight.

## Named References

- Studio Ghibli environmental art direction (Howl's Moving Castle + My Neighbor Totoro): color warmth, organic quality, sense of wonder
- Japanese craft aesthetics (wabi-sabi): beauty in imperfection, handmade quality
- Botanical illustration books: careful composition, warm palettes, organic forms

## Anti-References

- SaaS landing pages
- Brutalist developer portfolios
- Dark-mode tech dashboards
- Corporate enterprise sites
- Cottagecore aesthetics (too literal)
- Template portfolio kits

## Color Strategy

Committed. Forest green carries the brand (30-40% of interactive surfaces), supported by sky blue and golden amber warmth. Blossom pink appears only 2-3 times site-wide as a surprise.

## Typography Strategy

All-serif warmth: Vollkorn for display, Source Serif 4 for body. Libre Franklin (humanist sans) for UI labels and navigation. No monospace, no all-caps headings.

## Motion Strategy

Organic and gentle. Float rather than snap. Longer durations (800-1200ms), smaller translations (12px), softer ease curves. Motion should feel like a breeze, not a mechanism.

## Design Principles

1. Warmth over volume: communicate through tone and craft, not shouting
2. Personality is in the details: the font pairing, the color warmth, the gentle motion
3. Let the work breathe: generous spacing, clear hierarchy, unhurried rhythm
4. Human, not handmade-looking: avoid literal craft cliches while maintaining organic quality
5. Still an engineer's site: warmth should complement technical credibility, not replace it
